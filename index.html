About Me

I'm a passionate researcher at the intersection of architecture, energy, and urban design. My work explores how the built environment shapes our experience of spaces while optimizing for sustainability and energy efficiency. Through computational analysis and innovative methodologies, I seek to create architectural solutions that respond to both human needs and environmental challenges.

My Academic Journey

I am a graduate of Architecture and Energy with a focus on sustainable urban design and energy efficiency. My research examines how urban morphology impacts daylight availability, energy consumption, and solar potential in buildings and urban canyons.

I completed my Master of Science in Architecture and Energy at the University of Ilam, where my thesis investigated "The effects of heterogeneity in the layout and density of urban blocks on the daylight availability and energy efficiency of buildings." This research has laid the foundation for my subsequent publications in prestigious journals like Energy and Buildings and Sustainable Cities and Society.

My academic journey began with a BSc in Architecture Engineering from the University of Ilam, where I developed a strong interest in sustainable design principles. This early passion set the direction for my Master's and future PhD pursuits, shaping my focus on energy-efficient architectural and urban design.

Research Interests

  • Urban morphology and its impact on energy efficiency
  • Daylight availability in urban environments
  • Solar potential optimization in building design
  • Sustainable architectural and urban design
  • Computational methods in architectural analysis
