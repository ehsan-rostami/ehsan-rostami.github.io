The impact of urban morphology on sunlight availability at urban and neighborhood scales: A systematic review

Rostami, E., & Nasrollahi, N.

Sustainable Cities and Society, 121, 106194

DOI

This systematic review examines the relationship between urban morphology and sunlight availability across urban and neighborhood scales. The paper synthesizes findings from numerous studies to identify key morphological parameters that influence solar access and highlights methodological approaches for evaluating sunlight availability in complex urban environments.

A comprehensive study of how urban morphological parameters impact the solar potential, energy consumption and daylight autonomy in canyons and buildings

Rostami, E., Nasrollahi, N., & Khodakarami, J.

Energy and Buildings, 305, 113904

DOI

This study investigates the complex relationships between urban morphological parameters and their impact on solar potential, energy consumption, and daylight autonomy in urban canyons and buildings. The research provides insights into optimizing urban design for energy efficiency and daylight availability.

The impacts of urban canyons morphology on daylight availability and energy consumption of buildings in a hot-summer Mediterranean climate

Nasrollahi, N., & Rostami, E.

Solar Energy, 266, 112181

DOI

This paper examines how the morphology of urban canyons affects daylight availability and energy consumption in buildings located in hot-summer Mediterranean climates. The study provides valuable insights for urban planners and architects working in similar climate zones.
